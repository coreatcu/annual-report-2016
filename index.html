Ignite@CU

Ignite@CU brought entrepreneurial students from top universities across the country together for a weekend of education, inspiration and networking. At Ignite, we also built meaningful relationships between CORE and our counterpart student groups at Harvard, Penn and France’s Ecole Polytechnique to name a few.

Almaworks

Almaworks started as a simple idea: an accelerator program built by students for students. But over just the past year, it has expanded into becoming one of the most successful student-run accelerators in the country. Two cohorts of startups each engaged in a nine week intensive program which connected founders with mentors who could teach necessary and proven skills. The program ended with the Intercollegiate Pitch Off, which brought the best startups from schools from across the east coast to deliver their ideas in front of an audience of investors.

Startup Columbia

For the third annual instance of Startup Columbia, the university's two day entrepreneurship festival, CORE and its partners decided to focus on highlighting unconventional forms of entrepreneurship. The program features panels and talks from leaders in robotics, virtual reality, and broadcasting, and it also included a food pavilion consisting of the most innovative culinary entrepreneurs in the city. The festival ended on a high note, with Snapchat CEO Evan Spiegal delivering an inspirational story in an interview with CORE.

JADE

This past winter break, CORE collaborated with ADI to launch the first winter session for students at Columbia. JADE (Jumpstarting Aspiring Developers & Entrepreneurs) introduced first-year students to the world of startups and tech through an immersive week-long program, during which they learned beginner programming and met with various participants in the New York ecosystem.

Global Tech Treks

This past spring break, CORE airdropped five dozen students into three of the greatest cities across the globe—London, Paris, and Berlin—and put them face to face with some of the most exciting startups and founders within each location’s entrepreneurship ecosystem. Countless relationships were formed, and a cohort of students that wasn’t necessarily part of CORE’s traditional audience took away an entirely new understanding of how entrepreneurship works around the world.

Founders of New York

CORE Impulse launched it's biggest campaign yet in the form of Founders of New York. The project features in-depth interviews with some of the most exciting founders from New York, and it shows just how diverse an entrepreneur's path can be. Impulse members dove into startups in fields ranging from EdTech to BioTech, and the full scope of the project is only going to expand from here.
